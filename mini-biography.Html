John Dalton

Early Life

John Dalton was an English chemist and meteorologist best known for pioneering modern atomic theory

Born into a modest Quaker family in Eaglesfield, Cumberland, England, he was largely self-taught.

He began working as a teacher at age 12 and later ran a Quaker boarding school in Kendal alongside his brother.

He was also the 1stscientist to propose the formula of water

Important Achievements

Dalton's most groundbreaking achievement was formulating the atomic theory, proposing that all matter consists of indivisible atoms and establishing the first table of relative atomic weights./p>

Additionally, he discovered the law of partial pressures of gases and provided the first scientific description of colourblindness, a condition sometimes called daltonism.

Words that Inspire

John Dalton's words inspire because they highlight how humble, discipline persistence can unlock the universe's gratest secrets.

By stating,"If I have succeeded better than many who surround me, it has been chiefly—may I say almost solely—from universal assiduity,"this pioneering chemist and meteorologist proves that dedication matters more than innate genius.

He also once said that:

"Chemical analysis and synthesis go no farther than to the separation of particles one from another, and to their reunion. No new creation or destruction of matter is within the reach of chemical agency."

Correction Corner

Dalton proposed the formula of water as HO, but the correct formula of water now known is H2O
